Postpartum Depression

Postpartum depression affects approximately one in seven women worldwide, highlighting its significant impact on maternal mental health. Postpartum depression (PPD) is a mental health
condition that affects women after childbirth. It is characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, anxiety, and exhaustion that can interfere with daily functioning and bonding with the newborn. PPD typically occurs within the first few weeks to months after delivery, but it can manifest anytime during the first year. It is different from the "baby blues," which are short-lived mood swings experienced by many new mothers. PPD requires medical attention and support for proper diagnosis and treatment. Read More

Our Mission

PostCare, is to provide comprehensive support and empower individuals experiencing postpartum depression. We aim to create a safe and inclusive space where they can find understanding, connect with others facing similar challenges, and access valuable resources. Our mission is to reduce feelings of isolation and overwhelm, foster healing, and enhance the overall well-being of postpartum individuals. By raising awareness about postpartum depression and offering effective support mechanisms, we strive to make a positive impact on the lives of those affected, guiding them towards a brighter and stronger future .
